Great question — this one gets thrown around a lot, but most people only half-get it.

---

## ⚫ What “Shadow-Limited” Means

“Shadow-limited” is basically the same as **shadowbanned**, but with a softer edge. Instead of fully banning you, the platform:

* **Doesn’t tell you** anything’s wrong.
* **Quietly reduces your reach** so fewer people see your posts.
* Keeps you *technically active* (you can post, comment, etc.) — but your content is put on the back shelf.

---

## ⚫ How It Shows Up

You might be shadow-limited if:

* Your posts suddenly get **way fewer likes/views** than normal, even though you didn’t change style.
* People say they **aren’t seeing you in their feed** unless they search your name.
* Your hashtags don’t appear in search results.
* On TikTok, your videos stop hitting “For You Page.”
* On FB/IG, posts don’t leave your close circle of followers.

---

## ⚫ Why Platforms Do It

1. **Rule Breaking Lite**

   * You didn’t violate something hard enough for a full ban.
   * Example: edgy memes, “borderline” health claims, political takes.

2. **Advertiser Safety**

   * Like I explained before, if your content “scares advertisers,” they’ll just push you lower in feeds instead of outright removing you.

3. **Behavioral Punishment**

   * Posting too much, too spammy, too many hashtags.
   * Linking outside the platform too often (YouTube, Substack, etc.).

---

## ⚫ Why It’s Sneaky

* Platforms do this on purpose to avoid **bad PR**.
* If they banned you outright, you’d complain publicly and make noise.
* If they shadow-limit, you just think your content isn’t good enough — and most people blame themselves, not the platform.

---

⚡ **Bottom Line:**
Shadow-limited = you’re technically still allowed on the app, but the algorithm has *quietly turned down your volume knob*.
