Built By a Driver Who Got Tired of Bad Dispatchers

I drove trucks for 10 years. Over-the-road, long haul, seen it all. I had great dispatchers who understood the job, found good loads, and respected my time. I also had terrible dispatchers who booked garbage loads, violated my HOS, and made my life hell.

After 10 years on the road, I got tired of bad dispatchers hurting drivers. So I taught myself to code. Spent 1.5 years learning distributed systems, orchestration, AI, everything. Not to build a unicorn startup‚Äîto fix what's broken.

"Most dispatchers aren't evil. They're just ignorant. Nobody taught them the right way. OpenHWY teaches the right way. We train dispatchers to respect drivers, understand HOS, find good loads, and build real relationships. This is how dispatching should be done."

OpenHWY is open source (AGPL). Anyone can fork it, run it, modify it. I can't train all 3.5 million drivers myself‚Äîbut if others use this platform to train dispatchers, we fix the entire industry together.
